Help & Guide

Everything you need to know about using Gatekeeper to enforce trading discipline.

{# ── Glossary ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Glossary

Quick reference for terms used throughout Gatekeeper.

IdeaA potential trade you're evaluating. You work through a checklist before deciding whether to enter.
LayerOne of the 7 categories of rules in your trading plan (Context, Setup, Confirmation, Entry, Risk, Management, Behavioral).
RuleA specific condition within a layer. You define the rules; Gatekeeper enforces them.
Required ruleMust be checked before you can advance to the next layer. Skipping one blocks progression.
Optional ruleImproves your grade if checked, but won't block you from advancing.
Advisory ruleA reminder only. No effect on grade or advancement.
GradeA, B, or C — reflects setup quality, not profitability. A = 85%+ of checklist points, B = 65–84%, C = below 65%.
Discipline Score0–100 score measuring how consistently you follow your rules. Combines plan adherence, grade distribution, and violation rate. Aim for 70+.
Plan adherencePercentage of Required rules you checked before acting. 100% = followed every required rule.
Entry windowAn optional expiry time on an idea. Once it passes, the idea is automatically invalidated.
R-multipleHow many times your initial risk a trade made or lost. 2R = made twice your risk. −1R = lost your full risk.
SLStop Loss — the price at which you exit to cap your loss.
TPTake Profit — a target price at which you plan to exit with a gain.
BEBreakeven — moving your Stop Loss to your entry price. Worst case becomes a scratch (no loss).
Partials / Partial exitsScaling out of part of your position at a target. Locks in some profit while the rest runs.
PlanA named set of rules across the 7 layers. Only one plan is active at a time.
ViolationA Required rule that was not checked before you advanced or opened a trade.
{# ── Overview ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Overview

Gatekeeper is a trading discipline platform that enforces your trading plan through a structured, rule-based workflow. Every trade idea must pass through a 7-layer checklist before you're permitted to enter — preventing impulsive, off-plan trades.

Plan-first

Define your rules across 7 layers. Gatekeeper holds you to them.

Gated entry

You can only enter a trade after completing all required checks.

Track & improve

Journal every trade, review with AI, and monitor your discipline score.

{# ── Workflow ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Workflow

The core loop follows five steps, repeated for every trade opportunity:

  1. 1
    Spot an opportunity

    Create an idea with the instrument and direction (Long/Short). It starts in the Watching state.

  2. 2
    Work through the checklist

    Check off rules across the 7 layers. Your idea advances state only when the Required rules for each gate are met.

  3. 3
    Entry permitted

    Once your idea reaches Entry Permitted, open a trade with entry price, stop loss, and targets.

  4. 4
    Manage & close

    Track partials, move stops, and close the trade. Complete the Management layer to advance to Managed.

  5. 5
    Journal & review

    Write a journal entry reflecting on the trade. Use the AI coach for structured feedback, then check your reports.

{# ── The 7 Layers ─────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

The 7 Layers

Your trading plan is organized into seven layers. Each layer represents a distinct phase of trade evaluation.

1
Context

Market regime, higher-timeframe bias, news events. Is the environment right for this trade?

2
Setup

Pattern recognition, key levels, structure. Does the chart show a valid setup?

3
Confirmation

Trigger signals, momentum alignment, volume. Is there evidence the move is starting?

4
Entry

Precise entry criteria, order type, timing. Where exactly do you get in?

5
Risk

Stop loss placement, position sizing, risk-per-trade. How much are you risking?

6
Management

Trailing stops, partial exits, breakeven rules. How do you manage the open position?

7
Behavioral

Emotional state, revenge-trading checks, session limits. Are you in the right headspace?

Configure your layers at Trading Plans or use the Plan Builder to generate a custom rule set.

{# ── Rules & Checklist ────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Rules & Checklist

Each layer contains rules you define. Rules have three types that control how they affect state advancement:

Required

Must be checked before the idea can advance past the layer's gate. These are your non-negotiable criteria.

Optional

Tracked for grading but won't block advancement. Checking more optional rules improves your setup grade.

Advisory

Informational reminders. They appear on the checklist but don't affect grading or gating.

When viewing an idea, the checklist shows all rules for each layer. Toggle a rule on or off to record whether the condition is met. Required rules that are unchecked will block advancement — the system tells you exactly which rules are blocking.

Multiple Plans

You can create multiple trading plans — for example, one for trend following and another for mean reversion. Only one plan is active at a time. New ideas automatically use the active plan's rules. Existing ideas keep the plan they were created with, even if you switch the active plan later.

Manage your plans at Trading Plans. From there you can create new plans, duplicate existing ones, activate a different plan, or delete a plan. A plan cannot be deleted if it is currently active or if any ideas were created under it.

{# ── Trades ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Trades

A trade is created from an idea that has reached ENTRY_PERMITTED. Each trade records:

  • Entry price — where you entered
  • Stop loss (SL) — your risk boundary
  • Take-profit targets (TP) — one or more exit levels
  • Position size — how many units/lots

While a trade is open, you can:

Take partial

Close a portion at a target level

Lock breakeven

Move stop loss to entry price

Update stop loss

Trail your stop as price moves

Close trade

Record exit price and final R-multiple

{# ── Grading ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Grading

Every idea receives a setup grade based on how many rules (Required + Optional) were checked. The grade reflects the quality of your setup — not whether the trade won or lost.

GradeThresholdMeaning
A85%+ of possible pointsHigh-quality setup. Most or all rules checked. Your ideal trade.
B65–84%Good setup. Some optional rules missed. Acceptable but not textbook.
CBelow 65%Low-quality setup. Many rules unchecked. Discipline may have slipped.
A
A-grade

85%+ of checklist points. Most or all rules checked. This is your ideal trade.

B
B-grade

65–84%. Some optional rules missed. Acceptable but not textbook.

C
C-grade

Below 65%. Many rules unchecked. A warning sign that discipline slipped.

Grade reflects compliance with your rules — not whether the trade was profitable. An A-grade loser is a good trade that didn't work out. A C-grade winner is a discipline problem.
Over time, your Reports page shows grade distribution and R-multiples by grade — helping you see whether A-grade setups actually outperform.

{# ── Journal ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Journal

After closing a trade, a journal entry is automatically created. Use it to reflect on:

  • What went well and what didn't
  • Whether you followed your plan
  • Emotional state during the trade
  • Lessons learned for next time

Journal entries start as DRAFT and can be marked COMPLETED once you've finished your reflection. You can tag entries for filtering (e.g. "revenge-trade", "perfect-execution", "news-event").

{# ── Reports ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Reports

The Reports page gives you a data-driven view of your trading discipline:

Discipline Score

A 0–100 score measuring how consistently you follow your rules. Combines plan adherence (% of Required rules checked), grade distribution (how often you reach A and B setups), and rule violation rate. Aim for 70+. Higher means more disciplined.

Win Rate

Percentage of closed trades that were profitable (positive R-multiple).

Average R

Your average risk-reward multiple across all closed trades. 1R = you made back what you risked.

Plan Adherence

Percentage of Required rules checked before acting, averaged across all trades. 100% = you followed every required rule on every trade. Aim for 100%.

Consistency Trend

30-day chart showing how your discipline score changes over time.

Rule Violations

Which Required rules are most frequently left unchecked — your weak spots.

{# ── Plan Validation ──────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Plan Validation

Plan Validation checks whether your trading rules can be evaluated from historical OHLC price data. Run it from the Validation page (sidebar → Config → Trading Plans → Validate Plan, or directly at /validation).

Each rule is classified by the AI into one of three coverage categories:

OHLC Computable

The rule can be fully evaluated using price bars and standard indicators (moving averages, RSI, ATR, candlestick patterns, swing levels, etc.). These rules are candidates for historical replay.

Approximate

The rule can be partially captured from OHLC data, but with some loss of fidelity. An OHLC proxy is identified; the classification may not perfectly reflect your intent.

Live Only

The rule requires data or judgment that cannot be derived from OHLC bars — such as order flow, sentiment, or discretionary reads. These rules are enforced via the live checklist, not historical replay.

Interpretability Score

The percentage of your non-behavioral rules that are OHLC Computable or Approximate. A higher score means more of your plan can be historically tested.

Replay Readiness

READY — all non-behavioral rules have OHLC coverage. PARTIAL — some rules are live-only; replay will assume those layers are always satisfied. NOT_READY — no replayable rules, or the RISK layer has no testable stop rule (required to compute R-multiples).

Coherence Warnings

Deterministic checks on your plan structure: layers with no required rules, potential over- or under-filtering, and missing entry or risk rules.

Overriding AI Classifications

If the AI misclassifies a rule, you can confirm or override it via the JSON API (PUT /api/v1/validation/compiled-plans/{id}/rules/{rule_id}/confirm). Supply the correct status and, optionally, a list of the OHLC data sources required.

Phase 2 — running compiled rules against historical market data — is coming in a future release.

{# ── Plan Review ──────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Plan Review

Plan Review is an AI-powered analysis of your trading plan based on a sample of real trades and journal entries. Once you have completed and journaled enough trades, a "Plan Review" button appears on your trading plan page.

How it works

  1. You complete and journal a set of trades (default: 20, configurable in Settings → General → Plan Review Sample Size).
  2. Click Plan Review on your trading plan page.
  3. The AI analyses your plan rules, per-rule adherence, win rates when rules were followed vs. skipped, and your journal notes.
  4. A structured report is produced covering: overall verdict, per-rule performance, assumptions that held, assumptions that were challenged, and suggested plan changes.

Report contents

Overall Verdict

Keep — plan is working; no major changes needed. Refine — specific rules need adjustment. Overhaul — the plan's core assumptions are not holding up.

Rule Performance

For each rule: how often you followed it, your win rate when you did vs. when you didn't, and the AI's verdict (keep / review / remove).

Assumptions Held / Challenged

A plain-language summary of which plan assumptions the data supports and which it calls into question.

Suggested Changes

Specific, actionable suggestions for improving or simplifying your plan based on the sample.

Automatic review scheduling (triggered after every N trades) is planned for a future release.

{# ── AI Features ──────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

AI Features

Gatekeeper includes optional AI-powered features. Bring your own API key (OpenAI, Anthropic, or a local Ollama instance) in Settings (saved in the database). Alternatively, you can set ANTHROPIC_API_KEY or OPENAI_API_KEY in the environment (e.g. Docker Compose .env) — the app will use them when the database key is empty; Settings explains when a key comes from the environment.

Idea Review

Get an AI assessment of your idea's checklist — it highlights strengths, weaknesses, and potential blind spots based on your rules.

Journal Coach

After writing your journal entry, ask the AI coach for structured feedback on your trade execution, emotional management, and areas to improve.

Plan Builder

Use the Plan Builder to generate a complete trading plan through a guided conversation. Pick a strategy archetype (breakouts, pullbacks, mean reversion, etc.), timeframe, and market — or skip straight to a free-form chat. We'll create rules across all 7 layers based on your trading style. When you're done, you'll be taken directly to the active plan's detail page.

{# ── API & CLI ────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

API & CLI

Gatekeeper exposes a full JSON API and a command-line interface for automation and agent integration.

JSON API

All endpoints live under /api/v1/ and require a bearer token. Generate a token in Settings.

curl -H "Authorization: Bearer <token>" \
http://localhost/api/v1/ideas
CLI (gk)

The gk command provides the full workflow from your terminal.

gk ideas list
gk ideas create --instrument EURUSD --direction LONG
gk trades open --idea <id> --entry 1.0850 --sl 1.0800
MCP Server

Gatekeeper runs as an MCP server at /mcp, compatible with Claude Desktop, Cursor, and other MCP clients. AI agents can manage your full trading workflow.
