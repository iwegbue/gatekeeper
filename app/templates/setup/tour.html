Here's a quick overview of how Gatekeeper works. Bookmark this flow — it's how disciplined traders operate.

1. Spot an opportunity → create an {{ tip("idea", "A potential trade you want to track and evaluate. An idea records the instrument, direction (Long/Short), and your notes — before you commit to entering.") }}

Log a potential trade with the instrument and direction. It starts in the {{ tip("Watching", "The initial state. You've identified something worth tracking, but haven't validated the setup yet.") }} state.

Create your first idea →
2. Work through the 7-layer checklist

Check off each rule across Context, Setup, Confirmation, Entry, Risk, Management, and Behavioral layers. Your idea {{ tip("advances state", "Each state transition is gated by one or more layers. E.g. Watching → Setup Valid requires the Context layer to be complete.") }} only when required rules are met.

3. Entry permitted → open a trade

Once your idea reaches Entry Permitted, record the trade with entry price, stop loss, and take-profit targets.

View trades →
4. Close trade → write the journal entry

After closing, document what went well, what didn't, lessons learned, and your emotional state. The AI coach can give you structured feedback.

View journal →
5. Track progress in Reports

Monitor your {{ tip("discipline score", "A 0–100 score measuring how consistently you follow your plan — based on rule compliance, grade distribution, and win rate.") }}, win rate, {{ tip("average R", "R = Risk-Reward multiple. 1R means you made back exactly what you risked. Average R tells you your expected return per trade over time.") }}, and {{ tip("plan adherence", "The percentage of Required rules that were checked across all trades. High adherence = you're trading your plan consistently.") }} over time.
